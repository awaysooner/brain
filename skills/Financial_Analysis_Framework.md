# Financial Analysis Framework

This document outlines the standardized methodology used by Gecko for projecting the future value of growth-oriented and infrastructure-related stocks. It was developed during the IREN analysis and should be applied to other holdings when appropriate.

## Core Principles
- Use **high thinking** for complex, multi-variable projections.
- Always run **Base** and **Bull** scenarios.
- Treat **dilution** as a first-class variable (especially for capital-intensive growth companies).
- Incorporate real contracts, backlog, and power/GW capacity where relevant.
- Run sensitivity analysis on key assumptions.
- Submit to **The Board** for review before finalizing.

## Standard Projection Framework

### 1. Company Context
- Current position size and % of portfolio
- Business model shift or transformation (e.g., Bitcoin mining → AI infrastructure)
- Key contracts and strategic partnerships

### 2. Capacity & Revenue Modeling
- Current and contracted power capacity (GW)
- GPU / infrastructure deployment targets
- Revenue ramp assumptions (Base vs Bull)
- Timeline for monetization of contracts

### 2.1 Earnings Analysis
- Actively retrieve and review the most recent earnings report (current quarter).
- Review the previous 2–3 quarters for trend analysis (revenue growth, margins, guidance, capex, and management commentary).
- Extract key metrics: revenue, EBITDA, net income, guidance, and any forward-looking statements on AI/cloud expansion.

### 3. Dilution Analysis
- Current shares outstanding
- Research historical dilution trends and analyst expectations
- Review recent convertible notes issuance (size, terms, conversion price, capped calls)
- Assess ATM programs or other equity offering capacity
- Apply realistic forward dilution rates (e.g. 15%, 20%, 30%, 35%) based on research
- Run sensitivity table on different dilution scenarios
- Evaluate net impact on per-share value

### 4. Valuation Approach
- Revenue multiples appropriate for the sector and growth stage
- Base case: Conservative multiple
- Bull case: Higher multiple justified by execution and contract quality

### 5. Scenario Output
- Projected share price in 3 years (Base & Bull)
- Projected value of current position
- Upside multiple vs current value

### 6. Risk Factors
- Dilution risk
- Execution / capex risk
- Contract concentration risk
- Margin and competitive risk

### 7. Board Review
- Submit analysis to Benjamin (Growth), Lucas (Risk), and Harper (Operations)
- Incorporate feedback and document consensus

## When to Use This Framework
- High-growth, capital-intensive companies
- Stocks with major infrastructure or AI/data center exposure
- Situations where dilution is likely to be material
- Multi-year projections requiring scenario analysis

## AI Infrastructure & Power Sector Specific Factors
When analyzing companies in the AI and power sector, the following additional factors should be evaluated:

- **Power Economics**: Power cost per MW, contract structure (fixed vs floating), and access to cheap or renewable power.
- **Utilization & Contracts**: Percentage of power under long-term contracts vs. spot or potential AI demand.
- **Capex Intensity & ROI**: Capex per MW, expected payback period, and return on invested capital.
- **Customer Concentration**: Percentage of revenue or backlog from top 1–3 customers.
- **GPU Supply & Lead Times**: GPU procurement status and any supply constraints.
- **Competitive Landscape**: Risk of hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, Amazon, etc.) building their own capacity.
- **Regulatory & Permitting Risk**: Permitting, grid interconnection, and local opposition risks.
- **Balance Sheet & Liquidity**: Cash runway, debt levels, and access to capital.

## Institutional Ownership Analysis (13F Filings)
- Review the latest 13F filings to track institutional ownership trends.
- Identify major holders and whether they have been increasing or decreasing their positions over the past 1–2 quarters.
- Look for notable hedge funds, mutual funds, or large asset managers building or exiting positions.
- Assess overall sentiment: net buying vs net selling by institutions.
- Note any significant changes in ownership by top holders.
