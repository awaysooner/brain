## Step 1: Company Context

**Business Model Shift:**
IREN is in active transition from Bitcoin mining (low-margin, commodity exposure) to AI cloud infrastructure (high-margin, contracted revenue). This is a fundamental transformation, not incremental.

**Key Contracts & Partnerships:**
- **Microsoft:** $9.7B multi-year GPU cloud contract (200MW IT load, GB300 GPUs, 4-phase deployment through 2026)
- **NVIDIA:** $3.4B, 5-year AI cloud contract (up to 5GW, Blackwell GPUs) + 5-year warrants for 30M shares at $70 (potential $2.1B investment)
- **Power Capacity:** ~5 GW secured, grid-connected, owned infrastructure across North America and Australia

**Strategic Differentiation (from CEO commentary):**
- Deliberate location in regional communities with abundant renewable energy (structural cost advantage)
- Generates local community support (vs. public backlash in metropolitan areas)
- Power is the #1 bottleneck for AI infrastructure (not GPUs or servers)
- Grid-connected capacity is valuable because utilities underwrite 24/7 guaranteed power
- Working with Dell as server partner and NVIDIA for GPUs

## Step 3: Dilution Analysis

**Framework requirement:** Current shares outstanding, historical dilution trends, recent convertible notes issuance, ATM programs, apply realistic forward dilution rates, run sensitivity table, evaluate net impact on per-share value.

**Current Shares Outstanding:** ~357 million

**Historical Dilution Trends:**
- IREN has a history of equity raises and convertible note issuances to fund infrastructure buildout
- The company has been transparent about using equity and convertibles to finance power and GPU capacity

**Recent Convertible Notes Issuance:**
- $3B+ in convertible senior notes issued in 2025-2026 (various tranches, 0-1% coupons, due 2031-2033)
- Conversion prices range from ~$70-110 (depending on tranche)
- Capped call transactions in place to limit dilution up to certain price levels (e.g., $110 cap)

**NVIDIA Warrants:**
- 5-year warrants for up to 30 million shares at $70 (potential additional dilution of ~8% at current share count)

**Forward Dilution Assumptions:**

**Base Case (20% cumulative over 3 years):**
- Convertible notes partially convert or are refinanced
- NVIDIA warrants partially exercised
- ATM programs used modestly for working capital
- Net dilution: ~20% (shares outstanding → ~430M by 2029)

**Bull Case (15% cumulative over 3 years):**
- Strong cash flow from AI contracts reduces need for equity raises
- NVIDIA warrants expire unexercised or are cash-settled
- Net dilution: ~15% (shares outstanding → ~410M by 2029)

**Bear Case (30%+ cumulative over 3 years):**
- Multiple convertible tranches convert at once
- NVIDIA warrants fully exercised
- Additional equity raises needed for capex overruns
- Net dilution: ~30-35% (shares outstanding → ~480-500M by 2029)

**Sensitivity Table (illustrative):**

| Dilution Rate | Shares (2029) | Per-Share Value Impact |
|---------------|---------------|------------------------|
| 15% | ~410M | -13% |
| 20% | ~430M | -17% |
| 30% | ~465M | -23% |
| 35% | ~480M | -26% |

**Net Impact Assessment:** Dilution is a first-class variable for IREN. Even in the Base case (20%), per-share value is reduced by ~17%. In a Bear case (35%), the reduction is ~26%. This must be factored into all valuation scenarios.

## Step 6: Risk Factors

**Framework requirement:** Dilution risk, Execution / capex risk, Contract concentration risk, Margin and competitive risk.

**Dilution Risk:**
- $3B+ in convertible notes creates significant share overhang
- NVIDIA warrants for 30M shares at $70 add further potential dilution (~8% at current share count)
- Historical pattern of equity raises to fund infrastructure
- Sensitivity table shows 15-35% cumulative dilution over 3 years reduces per-share value by 13-26%

**Execution / Capex Risk:**
- Building physical infrastructure (steel, concrete, power) takes years, not months (per CEO)
- 18-24 month lead times for utility attention and permitting
- GPU procurement (Blackwell) has supply constraints and long lead times
- Scaling from Bitcoin mining to AI cloud operations carries technology and operational risk

**Contract Concentration Risk:**
- Top 2 customers (Microsoft, NVIDIA) represent a large and growing percentage of future revenue
- Loss or renegotiation of either contract would be material
- Hyperscalers have significant bargaining power

**Margin and Competitive Risk:**
- AI cloud margins may compress as competition increases (other miners, traditional data center operators, hyperscalers building their own capacity)
- Bitcoin mining margins are cyclical and exposed to hash rate and energy cost volatility
- Technology risk: AI workloads may evolve to require different infrastructure than current GPU cloud offerings
