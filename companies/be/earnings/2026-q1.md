# Bloom Energy Q1 2026 Earnings

**Source:** ~/brain/BE/earnings/Bloom Energy (BE) Q1 2026 Earnings Transcript _ The Motley Fool.pdf

## Summary

Bloom Energy delivered record first-quarter results with revenue more than doubling year-over-year to $751.1M (+130.4%), driven by explosive product sales to AI/data center customers. The company achieved its first positive Q1 operating cash flow in history (+$73.6M) and swung to GAAP net income of +$70.7M (from -$23.8M loss). Gross margins expanded and guidance for 2026 was materially raised (revenue $3.4–3.8B at midpoint implying ~80% YoY growth). The results validate the strategic pivot to onsite, grid-independent power for hyperscalers and utilities that cannot wait for traditional grid infrastructure.

Key highlights include 100% service attach rate on all deals (10–15 year average), manufacturing now adding capacity continuously at "hundreds of megawatts per quarter", an order-of-magnitude reduction in field installation time via skid-based solutions, and no order or capacity constraints.

## Key Segments / Highlights

- **Financial Turnaround**: Revenue $751.1M (product $653.3M), GAAP gross margin 30%, operating income turned positive, Adjusted EBITDA $143M (+468%), Non-GAAP EPS $0.44.
- **2026 Guidance Raised**: Revenue $3.4B–$3.8B (previously $3.1–3.3B), Non-GAAP gross margin ~34%, operating income $600–750M, EPS $1.85–2.25.
- **Cash Position**: $2.52 billion in cash at quarter end.
- **Operational Scale**: Current facilities support up to 5 GW annual output. Adding capacity in hundreds of MW per quarter on a continuous basis. "Not order constrained and not capacity constrained."
- **Installation Innovation**: "An order of magnitude reduction" in field installation time through skid-based solutions.
- **Service Economics**: 100% attach rate between product sales and service. "There is not a single deal that we do without an attach rate to our service."

## Deals & Contracts

- **Oracle Project Jupiter (New Mexico)**: Up to 2.45 GW microgrid — Bloom named sole power provider, replacing previously planned turbines and diesel generators. Part of expanded up to 2.8 GW relationship. Oracle invested ~$400M in BE shares (Oct 2025). Deployments delivered in 55 days vs 90-day target.
- **Brookfield**: Bloom designated preferred onsite power provider for global AI data center investments (up to $5B framework). First European site expected.
- **AEP**: 1 GW procurement + $2.65B, 20-year offtake for data center campus in Cheyenne, WY.
- **Nebius**: Long-term partnership up to 328 MW initial phase, monthly service fees over 10 years.

**Backlog Note**: Over half of current data center backlog from non-Oracle customers (other hyperscalers, neo-clouds, colocation providers).
