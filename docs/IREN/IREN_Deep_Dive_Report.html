1. Company Context

Iris Energy (IREN) is a vertically integrated Bitcoin mining and AI cloud infrastructure company. The company owns and operates data centers with significant power capacity, transitioning from pure Bitcoin mining to high-margin AI cloud services. Key assets include ~5 GW of secured power capacity across multiple sites in North America and Australia.

Business Model Shift: IREN is in active transition from Bitcoin mining (low-margin, commodity exposure) to AI cloud infrastructure (high-margin, contracted revenue). This is a fundamental transformation, not incremental.

Key Contracts & Partnerships:

  • Microsoft: $9.7B multi-year GPU cloud contract (200MW IT load, GB300 GPUs, 4-phase deployment through 2026)
  • NVIDIA: $3.4B, 5-year AI cloud contract (up to 5GW, Blackwell GPUs) + 5-year warrants for 30M shares at $70 (potential $2.1B investment)

Strategic Differentiation (from CEO commentary): IREN deliberately locates its facilities in regional communities with abundant renewable energy, creating a structural cost advantage. This approach also generates local community support, as opposed to the public backlash often faced by data centers in metropolitan areas. The CEO has emphasized that power—not GPUs or servers—is the primary bottleneck for AI infrastructure deployment, and that grid-connected capacity is particularly valuable because utilities underwrite 24/7 guaranteed power delivery.

6. Risk Factors

  • Dilution Risk: $3B+ in convertible notes creates significant share overhang; NVIDIA warrants add further potential dilution of up to 30M shares at $70. Sensitivity table shows 15-35% cumulative dilution over 3 years reduces per-share value by 13-26%.
  • Execution / Capex Risk: Building physical infrastructure (steel, concrete, power) takes years, not months (per CEO). 18-24 month lead times for utility attention and permitting. GPU procurement (Blackwell) has supply constraints and long lead times.
  • Contract Concentration Risk: Top 2 customers (Microsoft, NVIDIA) represent a large and growing percentage of future revenue. Loss or renegotiation of either contract would be material.
  • Margin and Competitive Risk: AI cloud margins may compress as competition increases (other miners, traditional data center operators, hyperscalers building their own capacity). Bitcoin mining margins are cyclical and exposed to hash rate and energy cost volatility.
  • Utility Relationship Risk: The CEO has noted that utilities are "risk adverse" and "inundated with requests." Any deterioration in these relationships could slow deployment timelines.
